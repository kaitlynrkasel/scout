# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People running outreach and relationship-building — music PR / playlisting, job &
internship seekers, networkers, and founders. Often solo or on a small team,
juggling many contacts at once. Context: at a desk, focused, doing repetitive
but high-stakes relationship work — finding the right people, writing
personalized messages, and tracking who replied. They want to move quickly and
to trust what the interface tells them.

Job to be done: find the right people and opportunities, send warm, personalized
outreach in their own voice, and see what's landing — without it feeling like
a chore.

## Product Purpose

Scout finds the right people/opportunities, drafts warm personalized messages in
the user's own voice, and tracks the pipeline (found → drafted → sent → replied).
Its judgment improves over time as it learns the user's taste (match quality /
deny rate) and what gets replies. The dashboard's job is to give a calm,
confident read on how outreach is going and what to do next. Success = the user
reaches more of the right people with less effort, and trusts Scout more each week.

## Brand Personality

Calm, confident, and quietly warm — a professional daily-driver tool with a light
human touch. Voice: plain, specific, and encouraging without being cute. Scout is
a capable assistant; the dog is a subtle personality cue, never the theme.
Emotional goal: trust and momentum. It should feel as dependable as a great
analytics dashboard, with just enough warmth to feel made by humans for humans.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI-SaaS slop:** cream + serif + terracotta palettes, gradient
  hero-metric cards, identical icon+heading+text card grids, a tracked-uppercase
  eyebrow above every section, gradient text.
- **Playful / childish / student:** notebook tape, ruled paper, cartoon warmth,
  over-rounded "bubble" components, mascot-forward theming.
- **Cold corporate / enterprise:** sterile all-gray, soulless, zero personality.
- **Dev-tool / terminal:** monospace-everything, code-editor chrome, overly
  technical readouts.

The needle to thread: Stripe-grade clarity and calm, plus a subtle, human warmth.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity is the feature.** Every screen answers "how's it going, and what do
   I do next?" at a glance. Data-forward, legible, honest.
2. **Calm confidence.** Restrained palette, one deliberate accent, purposeful
   space. Let hierarchy, typography, and good data display carry the design —
   not decoration.
3. **Warm, not cute.** Personality lives in the voice and a few small human
   details, plus a light Scout cue — never in theming or mascots. If a touch
   could read "childish," cut it.
4. **Earn every element.** No decorative or nested cards, no numbered/eyebrow
   scaffolding, no gradient text. Structure should encode real information.
5. **Show the work improving.** Surface Scout's learning (match quality, reply
   rate, trends) so the user trusts it more over time.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum: body text ≥ 4.5:1, large/bold text ≥ 3:1, placeholders held to
the same 4.5:1. Visible keyboard focus and full keyboard navigation. Respect
`prefers-reduced-motion` with crossfade/instant fallbacks. Never encode meaning
in color alone — pair status color with text or an icon. Use tabular numerals
for data so figures don't shift.
